Sensitive Organs

The skin around your eyes is the thinnest skin on your face. It is five times thinner than the rest of your face, and ten times thinner than that of other parts of the body. That means it has hardly any sebaceous glands and no supporting tissue, which is basically collagen. Therefore, it droops sooner.

This causes this area to break down more quickly, leading to wrinkles known as crow's feet, and eyelid sagging.

The impact of sun

We have just described that the part of the eyes is very sensitive since it is a very thin skin. Therefore, it has fewer layers underneath and, exposed to Ultraviolet rays. This results in what is known as hyperpigmentation. Another cause of this unfortunate coloration is genetics; there are simply people who naturally have more dark circles under their eyes than others.

Although fluid retention occurs to women in other parts of the body, in men this problem occurs in the eyes are. That’s when appear the feared dreaded bags as we get older.

Aging is another critical factor for us. When you are young, the membrane is responsible for retaining the fat in the eyelids. But when we gain weight or older, the eyes membrane gets thinner and loses strength over time,

How to protect them?

All in all, we can say that the skin around the eye contour is the thinnest in the human body, it has a small number of sebaceous glands and its blood microcirculation is very slow. As a result, it is fragile, dehydrates easily and accumulates toxins quickly. In addition, and due to constant muscle contractions, it is the first area where signs of fatigue or aging become visible.
