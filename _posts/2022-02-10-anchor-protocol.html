Introduction

In the recent years, we have been suffering a 0% interest rate policy in our deposits. In some cases, this has been translated even into negative interest into some countries or abusive custody or management fees. I have been always not happy about this topic, since we are lending our money to the banks (in some cases charging us for that money) and with that money, they lend it to other customers, for which they charge an abusive interest rate.

Although in the past decades there has not been any breakthrough in traditional finance (I just think about ATMs and credit cards), for the past years a new concept known as DeFi has been introduced and it’s getting more traction. Every year, there are several projects related to it, mainly linked to blockchains and different crypto currencies.

The purpose of this article is not praising the different benefits of DeFi and criticising the traditional finance. The goal is just to show that there are more alternatives to depositing the money in the bank, giving us a 0% on it.

What's Anchor?

For that, I am showing those who are not aware of Anchor Protocol. What’s this? Anchor is a platform that offers both savings account and a lending platform.

In this article we are just focusing on the savings account, for which depositors get approximately an APY of 19.5%.

The deposits are in TerraUSD (UST) , which is the Terra stable coin. The value of this coin is pegged to the USD and there is a mechanism to make sure that the value keeps pegged to the UST. There are some interesting videos about this algorithm and other projects such as White Whale that use it to get additional APY on the arbitrage. You can find addtional references in this link Useful links.

This means that with a deposit of 10,000 UST, we will get around 2,000 UST after one year. The UST is compounded daily and reflected in your deposit. For example, in one week, you get around 38 UST with the example above.

Please note that the APY slightly varies and it is likely the case that once there are several adopters of UST, the APY will reduced.

Anchor Protocol offers a flexible savings account with no minimum deposit and no commitment.

How to deposit into Anchor Protocol

We have said that the Anchor works with UST. You cannot deposit fiat or any other stable coins. Some months ago, it was more difficult to get UST. Fortunately, it is now more and more extended and there are more and more different options. The last exchange that listed UST was Kraken. In terms of fees, this may be also the most convenient one if you are living in Europe.

Before explaining briefly the different alternatives, you will need a Terra Station wallet. This wallet is the official one from TerraForm Labs and it’s used to manage the different Terra tokens (UST, LUNA, etc.)

There are some Chrome extensions and mobile applications for it. Once you have set it up, you will be able to send the UST (or LUNA) to your Terra Station address.

How to buy UST

There is an option to buy directly UST from Fiat in the Anchor protocol. The service is called Transact and it is displayed in the Anchor Protocol site. However, the conversion fees are quite high and there are cheaper options explained below.

Preparing the document, I have just seen that there is a new option (more and more exchanges are listing UST). This service is called Kado Ramp and the fee (2 UST) seems more affordable than Transakt.

Kraken

https://www.kraken.com/

Kraken is one of the most famous exchanges. In this case, the fiat deposit is free. You have options such as EUR and USD and you have pairs EUR/UST and USD/UST, meaning that you don’t have to convert to another coin to get UST.

The withdrawal fee to your Terra Station is 1 UST. The trading fees are quite low and the details are in the website. Expect around 1 UST per 1,000 EUR.

Binance

https://www.binance.com/

I cannot give my opinion about Binance US. The solution in Finance is depositing USD, and you get BUSD (the stable coin from Binance). This means that you are losing some part of the USD because the ratio is not exactly 1:1. Furthermore, you may also incur some transactions fees or exchange rate fees if your account currency is not quoted in USD.

From there, you can buy UST with BUSD. In this exchange, the withdrawal fee to the Terra Wallet is also 1 UST.

Coinbase

https://www.coinbase.com/

Please note that UST is also available in Coinbase, but this is not native UST and you cannot send it directly to Terra Station. You will need to convert it (e.g. using MetaMask) and this will incur high fees.

What you can do in Coinbase (Coinbase Pro for smaller fees) is either buying XLM (Stellar) or LTC (Litecoin) due to the low trading / transfer fees. You can send those to KuCoin and convert them to another stable coin such as USDT in order to convert them again to UST. The withdrawal fee in this case is 2 UST (it used to be 4 UST).

In Kucoin there are also SOL/UST pairs, so you could buy Solana in Coinbase and send it to KuCoin to the different transactions.

This option may be more expensive compared to the ones listed below.

Another option that exists is buying LUNA (TERRA) in any of the stock exchanges and sending it to your Terra Station. Terra Station has an option to swap LUNA / UST and the fee is 0.1%. This was the most convenient approach when UST was not listed in many exchanges.

What Anchor brings is a new concept for people tired of traditional banks and abusive rates. This is the crown of the jewell of the DeFi projects out there. Furthermore, this is a more conservative approach than buying crypto currencies, whose value fluctuates so much.

On the other hand, this is not a risk-free value and there may be some risks to be evaluated:

  • Smart contracts hack
  • Depeg of UST to USD
  • Losing the Wallet

My piece of advice is not putting all the eggs on the same nest, regardless of the reputation of the protocol or asset type. In the next weeks, I will introduce other DeFi protocols that could be interest to you.
